About Me
======
I am a PhD Candidate in Biomedical Informatics at the Division of Biomedical Informatics & Genomics, Tulane University, New Orleans. My research integrates multi-omics data — genomics, metagenomics, and clinical variables — with machine learning to build interpretable, clinically actionable models for osteoporosis and bone mineral density prediction.

My research trajectory began with work on machine learning prediction and the biological insight of HPV-related biomarkers. Building on this foundation of active research, I secured and coordinated a national research program in Colombia — funded by a grant from the Ministry of Science, Technology and Innovation (MinCiencias) — focused on identifying microRNA biomarkers for early cervical cancer detection. This program unfolded across two projects: the first identified diagnostic biomarkers in FFPE (formalin-fixed, paraffin-embedded) tissue samples, and the second extended this work to exfoliated cervical samples. Together, these findings were consolidated into a trade secret formally endorsed for intellectual property protection. I also completed research training at LSU Health Sciences Center (USA) and the University Medical Center Groningen (The Netherlands). My work bridges biostatistics, computational biology, and translational oncology, with an emphasis on scientific rigor, mentorship, and open collaboration.
